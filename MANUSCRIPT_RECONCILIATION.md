## 1. Relation-R hashing wording (§III-B.4a, §III-B.5, Appendix A.1) — INCORPORATED (V6.6)

**Was:** relation R proved `credID = SHA3-256(cred ‖ pk_issuer)` in-circuit, with
SHA-3-based Merkle non-membership at depth 32.

**Why it had to change:** measured — one Keccak sponge block is **239,176 R1CS
constraints** (`circuits/research/sha3_incircuit.circom`), so the `credID`
binding plus a depth-32 SHA-3 Merkle path exceeds ten million constraints,
irreconcilable with the reported ~21k.

**V6.6 text:** states that the in-circuit Merkle accumulator uses **Poseidon**,
and that the SHA-3 `credID` binding is an **off-circuit** check established by
(a) the wallet's Dilithium verification and (b) the verifier's on-chain
`pk_issuer` resolution + Poseidon-fold equality — not an in-circuit constraint.
Algorithm 3, §III-B.4a, and the §IV-B/IV-D assumptions/reductions are aligned.

## 2. Public-signal specification (§III-B.4a, Algorithm 3) — INCORPORATED (V6.6)

**Was:** prose listed five protocol inputs but Algorithm 3's `pub` listed only
four (omitted `stmt`); `pk_issuer` was carried as a 1,312-byte key.

**V6.6 text:** the five Groth16 public signals are specified as field elements,
in order `[ issuerKeyHash = Poseidon-fold(pk_issuer), revRoot, policyHash =
Poseidon(predicateCode, threshold), nonce, stmtCode = Poseidon(STMT_V1,
domainTag(verifierId)) ]`. Algorithm 3 now carries the five-element `pub`; the
verifier resolves the full `pk_issuer` on-chain and checks
`Poseidon(pk_issuer) == signal[0]`. Encodings are documented (uint32
range-checked `claims.age`; policy pinned by `policyHash`; verifier-domain
separation via `stmtCode`; verifier-chosen single-use `nonce`).
